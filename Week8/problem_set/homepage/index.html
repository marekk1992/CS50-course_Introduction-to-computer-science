FAQ (frequently asked questions)

What is a processor, and which ones are the best?
The processor, or CPU, is the bit of your computer that gets all the hard work done.
A high "clock speed" (which is how fast your CPU works, and is measured in GHz) is also good, but it's only relevant if you're comparing similar processors with a similar number of cores.
For Intel processors, you'll commonly see i3, i5, and i7. The bigger the number, the faster the processor will be â€“ generally, anyway.
What operating system do I need?
The operating system (OS) is basically the raw software that your computer runs on.
It controls how your computer works, what you see on the screen, and the content you have access to.
How much RAM or memory do I need?
RAM, or memory, is just where your computer stores everything it's currently doing.
If you're just browsing the web, 4-gigabytes is fine, but for streaming and casual gaming you'll want at least 8-gigabytes.
What sort of screen do I need?
What screen size you want depends on you.
Popular sizes are around 11 inches and 13 inches, because they're big enough to watch Netflix on, but keep your laptop's size down.
If you use a laptop a lot for work, it could be worth getting a 15-inch or 17-inch screen.
Would a tablet work in place of a laptop/desktop?
If the system is being used just for surfing, email and watching video then a tablet may be an option instead of a laptop or desktop system.
Again, the user will be able to help you answer that question.
How much do you want to spend?
This is a big factor and it often requires some help from you based on the answers you find from the questions above.
If you only want to spend a minimum amount but want a Mac then you may have to look for a used system.
The budget will go a long way toward narrowing down choices.
